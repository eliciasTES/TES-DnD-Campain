# Chapter 1 - Broken Route

## 1. The Briefing

Brynjolf presents the mission.

The objective is not to destroy the company.

The objective is to make the company appear unreliable, disorganized, and no longer worth financing.

Elias is given:

- A caravan worker's disguise.
- Forged shipping papers.
- A map of the compound.
- The informant's last known location.

He is reminded:

> "If they know the Guild was here, we've failed."

---

## 2. Reconnaissance

Elias spends the day observing the trading company from the surrounding hills and shoreline.

He studies:

- Guard patrols.
- Worker routines.
- Cargo deliveries.
- Shift changes.
- Warehouse entrances.
- Hidden escape routes.

He discovers the operation is larger and more organized than expected.

---

## 3. Infiltration

After nightfall Elias enters the compound unnoticed.

He blends in as a late-arriving caravan hand before slipping away from the workers.

Whenever possible he avoids detection.

If discovered, he quietly incapacitates isolated guards or workers without killing them, hiding them before anyone notices.

No alarms are raised.

---

## 4. Discover the Truth

Inside the office Elias uncovers:

- Shipping manifests.
- Route maps.
- Investor correspondence.
- Financial ledgers.

The operation is backed by wealthy investors rather than common bandits.

He also discovers the missing Guild informant alive inside the compound.

The chapter ends before Elias knows whether the informant has betrayed the Guild.

---

# Chapter 2 - Broken Route

## 5. The Informant

Elias secretly meets the informant.

He determines whether the informant is:

- Loyal.
- A traitor.
- Acting under coercion.

Their conversation reveals the true state of the rival organization.

---

## 6. Steal the Business

Instead of stealing merchandise, Elias steals information.

He takes:

- Customer records.
- Smuggling routes.
- Contracts.
- Account books.
- Investor correspondence.

Without these records, future shipments become chaotic and unreliable.

---

## 7. Quiet Sabotage

Elias carefully sabotages the operation without revealing outside involvement.

He:

- Switches cargo labels.
- Unlocks warehouse doors.
- Damages wagon axles.
- Cuts crane ropes after loads are secured.
- Loosens support beams.
- Spills lamp oil near stored supplies.
- Leaves crates stacked dangerously.

Hours after he leaves, the warehouse suffers a catastrophic "accident."

The losses appear to be the result of negligence rather than sabotage.

---

## 8. Escape

A worker notices something is wrong just as Elias leaves.

Rather than fighting, Elias disappears into the confusion.

He avoids pursuit using rooftops, the lakeshore, and hidden paths before reaching the Guild's extraction point.

By sunrise, no one can even describe the intruder.

Only that someone had been there.

---

## 9. Return

Brynjolf reviews the stolen documents.

The Guild now knows:

- Who financed the rival operation.
- Which merchants can be reclaimed.
- Which routes were compromised.

News arrives that the Lake Geir warehouse has suffered a disastrous accident.

Merchants begin abandoning the company.

Investors quietly withdraw their funding.

The rival organization starts collapsing under its own failures.

Satisfied, Brynjolf arranges Elias' long-awaited meeting with Maven Black-Briar.

The reward is not gold.

It is the information Elias has been searching for:

The location of Virel.
