# Brainstorming Thieves Guild Jobs

## Design Philosophy

The mission shouldn't be **impossible**—it should simply be a **bad fit for the Guild's best members**.

If Brynjolf, Vex, or Delvin could solve it in a single night, there is no reason to send the new guy.

Instead, each job should exploit one of the Guild's weaknesses while naturally highlighting one of Elias' strengths.

---

# V1: Eliminate a Rival Smuggling Operation ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Why the Guild Can't Do It

The rival gang knows the Thieves Guild.

They've memorized the faces of Brynjolf, Vex, Delvin, and several other members. Their hideout is full of lookouts specifically watching for known Guild operatives.

Sending a veteran would immediately expose the Guild's involvement.

Elias is an unknown outsider.

Even if someone notices him, they assume he's just another wandering mercenary or traveler.

### Why Elias Succeeds

- Unknown face
- Strong investigator
- Can pose as a hired sword
- Spellblade abilities if the mission turns violent
- Can quietly eliminate guards if necessary

**Overall feel:** Spy thriller mixed with organized crime.

---

# V2: Protect a Secret Meeting ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

### Why the Guild Can't Do It

The Guild isn't allowed to appear involved.

If Brynjolf or Vex are seen near the meeting, everyone immediately knows the Thieves Guild is protecting Maven.

Elias can work independently without drawing suspicion.

### Why Elias Succeeds

- Unknown to Riften's criminal circles
- Vigilant training makes him observant
- Patient investigator
- Able to notice inconsistencies and suspicious behavior
- Capable fighter if the assassination attempt succeeds

**Overall feel:** Detective story with infiltration and rooftop chase.

---

# V3: Recover a Guild Informant ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Why the Guild Can't Do It

One of Maven's spies disappeared after discovering valuable information.

Nobody knows if the informant:

- Was captured
- Betrayed the Guild
- Is working for another organization
- Is being coerced

Brynjolf wants the truth before acting.

Delvin personally recruited the informant, making him emotionally involved.

Brynjolf doesn't want loyalty clouding judgment.

### Why Elias Succeeds

- No personal attachment
- Objective investigator
- Good at gathering information
- Can infiltrate hostile territory
- Capable of making difficult moral decisions

**Overall feel:** Investigation with moral ambiguity.

---

# V4: The Haunted Strongbox ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

### Why the Guild Can't Do It

Every thief assumes the haunting is superstition.

Then they disappear.

Now nobody wants the assignment.

The Guild has no interest in losing more experienced members.

### Why Elias Succeeds

The Guild gets lucky.

They have no idea Elias trained with the Vigilants of Stendarr.

His experience with undead, cursed relics, and restless spirits gives him an advantage no ordinary thief possesses.

The Guild doesn't recruit him because of these skills—they simply discover them during the mission.

**Overall feel:** Supernatural burglary.

---

# V5: Destroy Evidence ⭐⭐⭐☆☆

### Why the Guild Can't Do It

Evidence linking Maven to numerous crimes has been seized.

However, the location is heavily guarded by Imperial officials.

Every known Guild member is already being watched.

If Brynjolf or Vex are spotted, it becomes an international incident involving the Empire.

### Why Elias Succeeds

- Completely unknown
- Can pass as a traveler
- Comfortable operating alone
- Spellblade abilities help overcome magical security if things go wrong

**Overall feel:** High-risk espionage.

---

# V6: Lost Shipment in the Ratway ⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

### Why the Guild Can't Do It

A valuable shipment vanished beneath Riften.

Everyone assumes ordinary bandits were responsible.

Experienced Guild members investigate...

...and never return.

The Guild suspects something magical has taken over part of the Ratway.

### Why Elias Succeeds

- Comfortable fighting when stealth fails
- Can investigate strange magical occurrences
- Strong enough to retrieve the shipment without needing backup

**Overall feel:** Urban dungeon crawl.

---

# General Guild Weakness

One of the Guild's biggest problems is simple:

**They're too recognizable.**

Brynjolf could explain it like this:

> "Every guard in Riften knows my face."

> "Every merchant knows Vex."

> "Half the city owes Delvin money."

> "You're the only one who can walk through the front gate."

Elias isn't valuable because he's stronger.

He's valuable because he's **invisible**.

# Combined Mission Idea (Favorite)

Instead of choosing between V1 and V3, combine them.

## Chapter 1

Brynjolf sends Elias to investigate a rival organization stealing Guild smuggling routes.

Because nobody recognizes Elias, he can infiltrate the operation without exposing the Guild.

During the investigation, Elias discovers the missing Guild informant.

The mission suddenly becomes much larger than expected.

---

## Chapter 2

Elias must decide whether the informant is:

- Loyal
- A traitor
- Being coerced

He rescues (or confronts) the informant, sabotages the rival headquarters so it appears to be an accident, escapes, and returns to the Guild.

Brynjolf realizes Elias has proven himself.

He arranges Elias' long-awaited meeting with Maven Black-Briar.

The reward isn't gold.

It's **information**—the location of Virel.
