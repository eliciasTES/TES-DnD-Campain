Elias Venym is a dark-skinned Imperial spellsword and wandering Daedra hunter whose life is defined by the search for truth rather than unquestioning faith. Raised as an ordinary citizen of the Empire, he joined the Vigilants of Stendarr believing that good and evil were simple opposites. While operating undercover inside a cult devoted to Namira, he discovered that many of its followers were ordinary people driven there by rejection, poverty, grief, and suffering rather than malice. After exposing the cult's corrupt leadership while sparing innocent followers, Elias became disillusioned with the Vigilants' rigid worldview and left the order.

Virel is a Dunmer shadowblade and former Tribunal acolyte whose life was shattered by a forbidden ritual that left her spiritually scarred and exiled from everything she once believed. Once a devoted servant of the Tribunal Temple, she became the victim of corruption hidden within its ranks, leaving her with fragmented memories, unstable shadow magic, and a deep uncertainty about her own identity. Though many consider her cursed, Virel refuses to let her past define her.

Elias Venym and Virel later travel Skyrim as wandering exiles with no permanent home, no allegiance to any kingdom or religious order, and no desire for fame. Though they come from very different backgrounds, both have been betrayed by the institutions they once trusted and now rely only on one another.

Elias gives Virel the secret book he store from the cult (the reason they pursue him with such intensity). He doesn't know the extent of its power or what secrets it holds but if he could not keep and protect it himself, he trusts her with the book more than the cultists. 

Elias told Virel he would head to Riften when he was better. Going there and searching for her was the only chance he had at finding the book again and to properly thank her for helping him.

His wounds are healed a few weeks after leaving Virel thanks to the kindness of an old lady named Anise and the refuse of her small shack in the woods of Falkreath.

Eventually he is led to Anises den in the mountains, she reveals herself to be a witch leading a trio of hagravens. Elias defeats them all, loots her treasure room and leaves the cave to continue on his journey. 

Elias has new gear he took from Anise's mountain cave den, leftover gear from his fight in the cave, and travel gear hidden near Anises cabin the woods.

His traveling pack includes a rolled bedroll, a full leather water-skin, a tinder kit of flint and steel, a fishing line, and a small iron pot and bowl.

He has a crude wooden bow and arrows, a torch, a mossy green and brown cloak Anise had gifted him, a coil of rope, several hunter's snares, and a small leather pouch filled with smooth, round pebbles, pungent alchemy ingredients, a few of Anise's mysterious small potion bottles, a pouch of silver and gold coins, and some humming soul gems. 

The spell books include, a hagravens book called "Darkside Utterings" filled with grotesque body manipulation alteration spells. A book written by hagravens who rule the forsworn of the reach called "Grimoire of the Briar Mothers" including spells and rituals of the Reach and the process of crafting Briar-hearts. A fire-based destruction spell tome called "Inferno". Including powerful custom spells written by Anise in the back pages and margins.

Elias also has Anises journal documenting her travels across Tamirel and her journey to becoming an evil witch.

Finally a sword. It was a one-handed hardened steel arming sword with a weathered, battle-worn finish. Thin silver runes are etched along the fuller, while the simple blackened iron crossguard, worn black leather grip, and polished raven-bone pommel give it a practical yet ominous appearance. The blade is lean, well-balanced, and built for a spellsword, emitting a faint magical hum that hints at a powerful enchantment. It hums with a enchantment but Elias was not sure what it did. 




We open the chapter with Elias back at Anises cabin now empty and quiet. He retrieves his travel pack hidden in the bushes nearby and takes some time to figure out what he can carry and what has to stay behind.
