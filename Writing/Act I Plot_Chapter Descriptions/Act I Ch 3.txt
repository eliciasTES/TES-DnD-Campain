# Journey Summary — Falkreath Woods

Elias Venym is a dark-skinned Imperial spellsword and wandering Daedra hunter whose life is defined by the search for truth rather than unquestioning faith. Raised as an ordinary citizen of the Empire, he joined the Vigilants of Stendarr believing that good and evil were simple opposites. While operating undercover inside a cult devoted to Namira, he discovered that many of its followers were ordinary people driven there by rejection, poverty, grief, and suffering rather than malice. After exposing the cult's corrupt leadership while sparing innocent followers, Elias became disillusioned with the Vigilants' rigid worldview and left the order.

Virel is a Dunmer shadowblade and former Tribunal acolyte whose life was shattered by a forbidden ritual that left her spiritually scarred and exiled from everything she once believed. Once a devoted servant of the Tribunal Temple, she became the victim of corruption hidden within its ranks, leaving her with fragmented memories, unstable shadow magic, and a deep uncertainty about her own identity. Though many consider her cursed, Virel refuses to let her past define her.


Elias Venym and Virel later travel Skyrim as wandering exiles with no permanent home, no allegiance to any kingdom or religious order, and no desire for fame. Though they come from very different backgrounds, both have been betrayed by the institutions they once trusted and now rely only on one another.

Neither fully trusts the other yet, but both recognize a familiar loneliness in the person beside them. They share little beyond quiet conversation, practical cooperation, and the understanding that neither can safely continue alone.

Throughout the journey, Elias searches for truth by studying the darkness he fights, while Virel seeks to reclaim the pieces of herself stolen by a forbidden Tribunal ritual. Their personal journeys evolve alongside one another, each helping the other heal in ways neither fully realizes. By facing ancient evils, difficult moral choices, and the burdens of their own histories together, they gradually discover that home is not a place they are trying to return to—it is something they are building with every step they take.

Their journey begins shortly after Virel helps Elias escape from a hidden cult devoted to Namira. During his investigation into the cult, Elias' true identity was discovered, forcing him to abandon months of undercover work and flee before the cult could silence him. Wounded and pursued, he survives only because Virel intervenes, guiding him through the forests of Falkreath Hold to a secluded campsite hidden far from roads and settlements.

Still wounded and hurt Virel draws the pursing Namiran Cultists away from where Elias lay broken and wounded. If she could keep them from discovering Elias by fleeing he might have a chance of escaping the woods and gain his strength. 

Elias gives Virel the secret book he store from the cult (the reason they pursue him with such intensity). He doesn't know the extent of its power or what secrets it holds but if he could not keep and protect it himself, he trusts her with the book more than the cultists. 

Elias told Virel he would head to Riften when he was better. Going there and searching for her was the only chance he had at finding the book again and to properly thank her for helping him.

His wounds are healed a few weeks after leaving Virel thanks to the kindness of an old lady named Anise and the refuse of her small shack in the woods of Falkreath.

Elias is plans to head to Riften soon. Anise knows this and makes up a story about her sister being taken by bandits to a cave not far to the south of the woods. Elias agrees to help her find her remains as a way of repayment for her kindness and help over the past few weeks. 

He is suspicious of Anise due to a change in her mannerisms. He agrees to help her because he want to believe that the teaching of the Vigilants, to beware honeyed works in secluded areas, was meant to breed distrust not vigilance.

Elias has a traveling pack hidden in the bushes near Anises cabin in case he has to flee. It includes a rolled bedroll, a full leather water-skin, a tinder kit of flint and steel, a fishing line, and a small iron pot and bowl.

The gear he takes to the cave includes a steel dagger, crude wooden bow and arrows, a mossy cloak Anise had gifted him, a coil of rope, several hunter's snares, and a small leather pouch filled with smooth, round pebbles, a torch wrapped in pungent pine pitch, a satchel of the weak potions and pungent ingredients, and a heavy oak branch bound in leather he would use as a club.

We open the chapter with Elias being led through the woods to the cave Anise says her sister was taken. He recognizes the signs of darker creatures than wolves or trolls as they get closer which makes him question Anises story about bandits being the culprits even more. Elias has no idea it is the den of her masters, vicious deadly hagravens.  

When they get to the entrance of the cave he tells her to head back to her cabin and he would meet her there. She pretends to leave as he enters but circles back when he is gone. She was expected to bring the sacrifice herself so using her knowledge of the cave to go through secret passages inside the cave behind Elias.
