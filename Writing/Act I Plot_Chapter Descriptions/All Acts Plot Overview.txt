# Elias and Virel in Skyrim

There are about 5-6 chapter per part around 10 to 12 per act. I use real NPCs and locations from Skyrim. Elias and Virel are the only made up characters so far. The story takes place at the same time as the Skyrim video game though neither Elias or Virel is the Dragonborn. The event of the main questline occur during their journey but most of the side quests are left untouched allowing Elias and Virel to affect the guilds in their own unique way.

I - Question/Conflict 
II - Complication/Escalation
III - Answer/Resolution







## Act I - Survival and Connection

Elias loses everything, meets Virel, and discovers something is wrong with him.

### Pt. 1 Traveling in the Wilderness

Elias steals a sacred book from the elders of the cult of Namira at their hideout in the Reach. They send low level cultists hunt him as he flees westward. They finally catch up to him on a road in the woods of Falkreath. Elias is saved by a mysterious Dunmer shadowblade named Virel after being critically wounded by Namiran cultists. She takes him to temporary safety in the wilderness, prevents him from dying with her healing magic and draws the cultists away from him so he has a chance to escape and recover his strength. Before they part Elias tells her he would make his way to Riften if he manages to survive. Elias spends time in Falkreath as he heals, makes his way past Helgen seeing the wake of Alduin's attack on the city, and eventually makes it to the Rift. 


### Pt.2 Arrival in the Rift/Riften


When he arrives in the Rift he begins having visions, dreams, hears voices. He doesn't know it at this point but Namira has chosen his as her new champion and closely watches his journey waiting until he has drifted far enough from his original teachings of the Vigilants in order to effectively use power from both the light and the dark side.

When he makes it to Riften he begins a long-odds search for clues in the to find out if Virel ever arrived to reunite with him. He eventually is forced to accept help from the Thieves Guild and the powerful Maven Black-Briar. After doing work for them Maven and Brynjolf reveal to Elias that a powerful Dumner shadowblade dressed as a priestess was caught with a powerful book owned by the elder followers of Namira. She was captured and sold to The Caller. He gets a location- Fellglow Keep and races off to try and save her. Maven keeps things a mystery but she orchestrated Virel's capture and sells her to the Caller.

Elias travels to Fellglow Keep to find her. He discovers her being used as a conduit for a dangerous ritual which Elias interrupts but not before the magical energy causes her mind to be damaged. Elias saves and helps her to recover similar to how she helped him at their first meeting. The Caller's ritual leaves her with fragmented memories, unstable shadow magic, and a deep uncertainty about her own identity. The one person she can remember and trusts despite their history spanning only one day is Elias. 

The Caller was not trying to kill Virel. She was trying to achieve something more: a memory restoration ritual, a soul extraction ritual, a Daedric communication spell

Namiran book can include 

- previous champions
- rituals used to bind champions
- weaknesses of Daedric influence
- forgotten conflicts between Namira and other Princes

The book contains dangerous knowledge and magic that the Caller wants for herself.





## Act II - Discovery and Transformation

Elias and Virel search for answers while both are changed by forces beyond their control.

Elias: namira's connection becomes stronger, he can sense souls, he hears whispers more clearly, he starts seeing things others cannot. The ritual accidentally creates the perfect condition for Namira to claim him.

Virel: her shadow magic behaves differently, she accidentally uses magic she doesn't remember learning, she recognizes people/places she shouldn't, she has instincts from her old life but no memories attached to them



### Pt. 1

Elias and Virel travel Skyrim together on a quest to try and fix her damaged mind and find Elias' place in Skyrim.

Every guild answers one question about either Elias or Virel.

They Travel to: 

Winterhold/College → Whiterun/Kynareth → Azura → Riften/Dawnguard


1. The College of Winterhold: "What happened to Virel?" The problem is magical. The College immediately gives them a goal.

Search for info on the ritual and magical cures. The Caller is an expelled student of the college.

Savos has his own history with dangerous magical experiments. He understands the consequences of sacrificing people for magic.

Tolfdir Would be sympathetic. He is fascinated by ancient magic but cautious.

The College raises the question: "Is there some knowledge that is not meant to be recovered?"


2. Temple of Kynareth in Whiterun. Healing memories. 

Kynareth represents: life, breath, nature, the soul's connection to the body 

Kodlak's storyline mirrors Virel. The Companions can teach her: You are not your curse. Kodlaks concern is "What happens to me after death?"

The Companions/Kynareth raises the question: "At what point do the spirit body and soul become different entities/separate?", "Can someone be separated from their curse?"


3. Temple of Azura. 

Dunmer history for Virel. Azura's Star/The Black Star are important for healing her mind. The ritual was related to soul trapping.

Azura represents: identity, prophecy, transformation, memory

The Black Star can help heal Virel but using it requires Elias to make a morally questionable choice.

Temple of Azura raises the question: "If you recover your past, will you accept who you were?", "If you could restore everything you lost, would you still be the same person?"


4. Dawnguard. Connection with Meridia. Appearance of Serana. Ties back into soul trapping and the Soul Cairn. Return to Riften. Confront Maven for using Virel as a good to be sold.

The Soul Cairn raises the question: "What happens when a soul is separated from itself?", "Is a soul defined by memories, choices, or something deeper?"

Serana would immediately understand Virel. She understands: waking up in a changed world, losing centuries of life, being used by family, questioning identity. Serana's story is about choosing who she is. She asks Virel: "You think losing your memories means losing yourself? I spent centuries trying to forget mine."









### Pt. 2

Virel's mind is healed and she gets a a power upgrade. 

She remembers who she was.
She doesn't like who she was.
Memory does not equal identity.

1. Old Virel/Before Fellglow Keep: Highly disciplined, suspicious, independent, emotionally guarded, pragmatic, willing to sacrifice herself or others for a mission, believes attachment is a weakness, defines herself through survival.

"People are temporary. Skills are permanent. Depend on yourself."
"I survive because I trust nobody."

2. Cursed Virel/After Fellglow Keep: Curious, vulnerable, more willing to trust, more compassionate, less afraid of needing others, more willing to ask questions, more emotionally honest, closer to Elias
 
Loses her: grudges, trauma, old obligations, expectations, identity built around her past

The curse should remove the barriers preventing her from being kinder.

"I don't know who I was, but I know who I want to be."
"I survive because someone believes in me."

3. Restored Virel: cold, more ruthless, isolated

Remembers her: betrayals, losses, mistakes, enemies, trauma, the choices she made, identity built around her past, grudges, trauma, old obligations, expectations.

"I wasn't cold because I was empty. I was cold because I survived."
"I survive because I choose who I trust."


Virel realizes Elias liked the person she became after losing her memories.

She has to ask herself: "Do I want my old self back, or do I want to become someone new?"


Elias thinks: "When she got her memories back, I lost her."

Actually He never met the "real" Virel. He met a version of her. She has to choose whether that version is worth keeping.

The journey to save Virel ends. Elias' purpose of helping Virel is fulfilled and he is forced to focus on himself and deal with Namira's influence on him.






### Pt. 3

Namira fully reveals herself to Elias and he gets a power upgrade along with the responsibilities that come with being the champion of a Daedric Prince. 

Namira to Elias:
"You spent this entire journey fighting to save one person."
"You call my gifts corruption. Yet every time you were afraid, you reached for them."
"You already chose me."

Elias gets the Namira's weapon a one handed sword like the Dawnbreaker. It consumes magic and corrupts the living. Meridia purifies/cleanses while Namira corrupts/consumes

What Elias loses in Act II:

- The Vigilants declare him corrupted and a priority target.
- Namiran Cultists see him as a affront to their control and place with Namira and deem him priority target.
- Elias realizes Namira manipulated the entire journey.
- Virel fears what Elias has become.
