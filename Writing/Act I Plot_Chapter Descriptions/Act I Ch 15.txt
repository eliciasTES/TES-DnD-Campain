Elias Venym is a dark-skinned Imperial spellsword and wandering Daedra hunter whose life is defined by the search for truth rather than unquestioning faith. Raised as an ordinary citizen of the Empire, he joined the Vigilants of Stendarr believing that good and evil were simple opposites. While operating undercover inside a cult devoted to Namira, he discovered that many of its followers were ordinary people driven there by rejection, poverty, grief, and suffering rather than malice. After exposing the cult's corrupt leadership while sparing innocent followers, Elias became disillusioned with the Vigilants' rigid worldview and left the order.

Virel is a Dunmer shadowblade and former Tribunal acolyte whose life was shattered by a forbidden ritual that left her spiritually scarred and exiled from everything she once believed. Once a devoted servant of the Tribunal Temple, she became the victim of corruption hidden within its ranks, leaving her with fragmented memories, unstable shadow magic, and a deep uncertainty about her own identity. Though many consider her cursed, Virel refuses to let her past define her.

Elias Venym and Virel later travel Skyrim as wandering exiles with no permanent home, no allegiance to any kingdom or religious order, and no desire for fame. Though they come from very different backgrounds, both have been betrayed by the institutions they once trusted and now rely only on one another.




Elias makes his way through the Ratway and manages to find the Ragged Flagon. After becoming acquainted with the prominent members of the guild Vekel the Man, Vex, Delvin and, Brynjolf he learns that Maven Black-Briar, the most dangerous person is Riften, had a run in with a Dunmer woman that could be Virel.

The Thieves guild members agree to set up a meeting with Maven so Elias can find out if the woman is Virel and where she is now, however Elias first has to pull a job for them and earn the trust of the guild. The mission is for him to investigate and disrupt a rival organization stealing Guild smuggling routes. 

Elias learns on the mission that the rival organization was handling massive weapons and gear shipments to both the Stormcloaks and the Imperials, an amount that would escalate the war and create a tremendous amounts of destruction and death across Skyrim. Maven Black-Briar wants to be the one profiting off the war.

After Elias successfully completes the job, securing Riften and the Black-Briars as the dominant shipping and smuggling authority in the East. He goes back to the Thieves guild, gets escorted to Black-Briar manor by Maul, Maven's right hand man, where he will meet with Maven.

At their meeting Maven recognizes Elias as a capable and unusually perceptive operative. She keeps the conversation completely professional, never intimidated or openly hostile. When Elias asks about the Dunmer woman, Maven never admits any wrongdoing. Instead, she carefully tells only technically true statements.

She explains that Virel had become an extremely valuable target, pursued by powerful organizations across Skyrim. Before anyone in Riften could decide what to do with her, a mysterious mage known as The Caller paid an enormous price to acquire her. Maven presents herself as nothing more than an intermediary who became aware of the transaction after it was already in motion. She never says she captured, imprisoned, or sold Virel, only that she knows where she was taken.

Maven gives Elias the only lead she has: The Caller's identity, the direction she traveled, and enough information for Elias to track her down. She deliberately creates a sense of urgency, reminding him that every moment spent questioning her is another moment Virel slips farther away. Elias realizes that rescuing Virel is more important than continuing to interrogate Maven and leaves Riften immediately.

Unknown to Elias, Maven has carefully manipulated the conversation. She intentionally omitted that she was the architect behind the deal—using her influence to arrange Virel's capture, recognizing her value because of her connection to the Tribunal, negotiating the sale to The Caller, and profiting immensely from it. Elias will not discover Maven's true role until much later, when The Caller reveals the full truth, turning what once seemed like a simple business transaction into one of the greatest betrayals of Elias's journey.



We open the chapter with Elias entering Black-Briar manor. He is finally going to meet Maven and ask her about what she knows about Virel. His weapons are confiscated at the front door by her guards.
