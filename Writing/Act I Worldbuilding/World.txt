# World Summary — Skyrim at the Dawn of an Uncertain Age

The story takes place in Skyrim shortly before the events of *The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim*. The dragons remain entombed in their ancient burial mounds, their return unknown to the people of Tamriel. The province stands on the edge of upheaval, but most believe life will continue much as it always has.

The civil war between the Imperial Legion and the Stormcloak rebellion has begun but has not yet erupted into full-scale conflict. Political tension is felt in every hold as jarls quietly choose sides, soldiers prepare for the possibility of war, and ordinary citizens grow increasingly divided over questions of loyalty, tradition, and the Empire's authority. Rumors spread faster than armies, and every tavern has its own version of the truth.

The White-Gold Concordat continues to shape daily life. Open worship of Talos is officially forbidden under Imperial law, though enforcement varies from hold to hold. Many Nords quietly honor Talos in secret, while others resent both the Empire and the Aldmeri Dominion for imposing foreign rule upon Skyrim's traditions. The Dominion itself maintains only a limited visible presence, but its influence is felt through diplomacy, politics, and fear rather than open occupation.

Beyond the cities, Skyrim remains a harsh and dangerous land. Ancient Nordic ruins, Dwemer cities, forgotten temples, abandoned forts, and hidden caves dot the wilderness, many untouched for centuries. Bandits prey upon travelers, necromancers pursue forbidden knowledge in isolated ruins, vampires stalk forgotten roads, and scattered Daedric cults continue their secret worship far from civilization. Most people dismiss such cults as isolated fanatics, unaware that many survive by blending into ordinary society.

Religion remains an important part of everyday life. Temples dedicated to the Divines provide healing, guidance, and sanctuary, while the Vigilants of Stendarr travel the province hunting Daedra, vampires, werewolves, and practitioners of forbidden magic. Though respected by many, the Vigilants are not universally trusted, as their uncompromising methods sometimes place suspicion upon innocent people caught too close to the supernatural.

Magic occupies an uneasy place within Skyrim. The College of Winterhold continues its studies despite widespread distrust from the local population, who often blame magic for the Great Collapse that devastated the city years earlier. Many Nords view mages with caution, tolerating their usefulness while remaining wary of their power.

For most citizens, life revolves around survival. Farmers tend isolated homesteads, miners work deep beneath the mountains, merchants brave dangerous roads between holds, and hunters disappear into forests where ancient things still linger. News travels slowly, superstition is common, and the line between history and legend is often blurred. Many dismiss tales of Daedric artifacts, forgotten heroes, and ancient evils as stories told to frighten children—until they encounter them firsthand.

Against this backdrop, Elias Venym and Virel travel as independent wanderers. They are not heroes recognized by the people of Skyrim, nor are they chosen to shape the fate of kingdoms. Their journey unfolds in the forgotten places between cities, where hidden cults, abandoned shrines, cursed ruins, and overlooked tragedies reveal that the greatest threats to Tamriel often grow unnoticed in the shadows long before history records their names.
