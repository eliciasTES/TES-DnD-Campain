# Enthir — Character Sheet

## Overview
**Race:** Bosmer (Wood Elf)  
**Class:** Arcane Merchant / Black Market Enchanter / Dwemer Relic Broker  

**Alignment:** Neutral (leaning Lawful Neutral in commerce, Chaotic Neutral in ethics)  

**Affiliations:**
- College of Winterhold (Member / Arcane Vendor)
- Thalmor Black Market Trade Network (Indirect Historical Contacts)
- Independent Enchantment & Relic Exchange Circles
- College Auxiliary Procurement Network

**Notable Quirks:**
- Treats rare artifacts like stock inventory rather than sacred objects  
- Speaks casually about highly dangerous magical items  
- Constantly evaluates conversations in terms of profit potential  
- Never reveals full knowledge of where his goods originate  

**Playstyle:**
Support economy archetype specializing in enchanted goods, rare artifacts, information trade, and magical resource distribution. Functions as a “quiet power broker” within mage and adventurer ecosystems.

- Slim Bosmer build with sharp, calculating facial features  
- Well-groomed appearance that suggests controlled professionalism rather than combat readiness  
- Calm, half-lidded eyes that constantly assess value in surroundings  
- Fine merchant robes layered with subtle enchantment protections  
- Always appears composed, even when discussing dangerous or illegal artifacts

**Armor / Attire Design:**

## Backstory: “The Man Who Learned the Price of Everything”

Enthir did not arrive at the College of Winterhold seeking enlightenment. He arrived seeking access.

From a young age, he showed little interest in the traditional Bosmer relationship with nature or tradition. Instead, he gravitated toward systems—how value is assigned, how scarcity is created, and how knowledge becomes leverage. Magic, to him, was never mystical in the spiritual sense. It was a resource: unstable, powerful, and poorly regulated in most of Tamriel.

Before the College, Enthir spent years moving through independent trade circles that operated between Skyrim, High Rock, and Hammerfell. These networks were not formal guilds, but loose associations of merchants, smugglers, and enchanters who understood that magical artifacts were more valuable when their origins were unclear. It was here that Enthir developed his defining skill: translating danger into price.

By the time he reached Winterhold, he was already functioning as a broker rather than a student. He did not pursue the College for academic recognition, but for proximity to controlled magical production. The College represented something rare in Skyrim: a concentrated source of enchantment, relics, and arcane experimentation that could be monitored, acquired, and redistributed.

Enthir quickly positioned himself not as a traditional mage, but as an intermediary between knowledge and those willing to pay for it. While others studied spells, he studied demand. While others cataloged artifacts, he cataloged buyers. Over time, he became the College’s most reliable—if morally ambiguous—supplier of rare and restricted magical goods.

His relationship with the College is fundamentally transactional. He does not challenge its authority, nor does he fully align with its academic ideals. Instead, he occupies the space between institutional control and external markets, ensuring that valuable items do not remain stagnant within sealed vaults or forgotten ruins.

Enthir’s neutrality is not philosophical—it is structural. He operates under the belief that everything has a price, even knowledge that others consider sacred or forbidden. This belief has allowed him to navigate dangerous trade networks, including those involving Thalmor-adjacent artifacts, Dwemer relics, and other items most institutions prefer to pretend do not exist.

Despite his detachment, Enthir is not reckless. He understands that the value of a network depends on its stability, and he is careful not to disrupt the systems he profits from. This makes him more of a stabilizer than a destabilizer within the College’s broader ecosystem, even if his ethics remain questionable.

In the context of Elias and Virel’s journey, Enthir represents the infrastructure of hidden knowledge. He is not a seeker of truth or power, but the mechanism through which both circulate. If something is rare, dangerous, or forgotten, he likely knows who wants it—and what they are willing to trade in return.

He does not judge what others seek. He simply ensures it can be obtained.
