# Farkas — Character Sheet

## Overview
**Race:** Nord  
**Class:** Companion Warrior / Beast-Blood Bruiser / Shielded Vanguard  

**Alignment:** Neutral Good  

**Affiliations:**
- The Companions (Circle Member)
- Harbinger Kodlak Whitemane (Loyal follower)
- Werewolf Bloodline (Beast-Blood bearer)
- Whiterun Guard Auxiliary (Informal cooperation)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Speaks plainly and struggles with abstract concepts or politics  
- Often pauses before responding, as if translating thought into words  
- Trusts strength and kindness more than logic or persuasion  
- Shows discomfort when forced into deception or manipulation  

**Playstyle:**
Tank frontline bruiser specializing in sustained melee combat, defensive engagement, and beast form endurance fighting. Excels in holding space, absorbing damage, and overpowering enemies through raw physical resilience.

## Appearance
- Large, heavily built Nord with natural warrior physique  
- Short-cropped hair and rough beard, minimally maintained  
- Soft but tired eyes that contrast with his physical strength  
- Heavy Companions armor reinforced for frontline durability  
- Presence feels imposing but not aggressive unless provoked

## Character Design / Theme

A warrior of immense strength constrained by a gentle and uncertain mind.

**Silhouette:**
- Broad, heavy frame designed for endurance and impact resistance  
- Stable stance that suggests defensive readiness rather than aggression  

**Armor / Attire Design:**
- Heavy Companions steel armor reinforced for frontline combat  
- Fur-lined shoulders and rugged leather bindings  
- Functional design prioritizing protection over mobility or elegance  
- Slight asymmetry in wear indicating frequent shield-breaking combat  

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Steel gray  
- Accent: Muted brown leather  
- Secondary: Wolf-white fur tones  

**Signature Details:**
- Hands often clenched or resting at sides due to restrained strength  
- Armor shows repeated impact marks from sustained combat roles  
- Beast transformation manifests as overwhelming physical force rather than precision

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Kind-hearted and non-confrontational in speech  
- Simple, direct thinker with limited abstract reasoning  
- Loyal to those who show him respect and patience  
- Prefers clear instructions over ambiguous goals  

**Motivations:**
- Protect the Companions and those he considers family  
- Avoid unnecessary violence whenever possible  
- Prove that strength does not require cruelty  
- Maintain stability through dependable presence rather than leadership  

**Flaws:**
- Low confidence in decision-making outside combat  
- Easily manipulated due to trusting nature  
- Struggles with complex moral or political situations  
- Emotional repression leading to internal confusion under stress

## Backstory: “Strength That Never Learned to Speak Loudly”


Farkas was born in Skyrim and raised within the Companions of Jorrvaskr, where strength is expected but understanding is not always required. From a young age, he was trained in combat alongside his brother Vilkas, but where Vilkas adapted through structure and discipline, Farkas developed through instinct and repetition. He learned by doing rather than analyzing, and by enduring rather than refining.

Within the Companions, Farkas became known for his reliability in straightforward situations. He does not overthink conflict or attempt to interpret it beyond what is immediately in front of him. His strength is direct, physical, and consistent, making him dependable in battles where clarity is more important than precision. In a place like Jorrvaskr, where warriors often define themselves through ideology or reputation, Farkas remains grounded in action rather than interpretation.

Farkas is also one of the Circle, bound to the beast blood of lycanthropy. Unlike others who struggle with the transformation or attempt to control it through discipline, Farkas experiences it in a more instinctive way. He does not frame it as curse or empowerment, but as something that simply exists within him. This lack of abstraction allows him to move between states without the same internal resistance others experience, though it also limits his ability to reflect on it deeply.

Because of this, Farkas often serves as the most physically dependable member of the Companions, especially in situations where hesitation would create risk. He is not concerned with political nuance, long-term consequence, or layered interpretation of events. His focus remains on the immediate task and the safety of those around him in the moment.

Despite his strength, Farkas is not driven by ambition or dominance. He does not seek leadership within the Companions and is often more comfortable following clear direction than defining it himself. His sense of loyalty is simple and direct, formed through shared experience rather than ideology. Once trust is established, it is rarely questioned.

His relationship with his brother Vilkas defines much of his understanding of the world. Where Vilkas analyzes, Farkas acts. Where Vilkas questions structure, Farkas accepts it as long as it functions. They complement each other without needing to mirror each other, forming a balance between thought and action within the Companions.

In the broader world, Farkas does not concern himself with complex political or metaphysical conflicts. However, he is often present in situations involving individuals who exist outside normal patterns of behavior or intent. Figures like Elias Venym and Virel are not fully understood by him, but he responds to them based on immediate intent rather than long-term interpretation.

Farkas does not try to define the world. He simply meets it as it is, one moment at a time.
