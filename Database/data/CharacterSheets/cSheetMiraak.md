🐉 Miraak – The First Dragonborn

> *“You think you are chosen. I was claimed before choice existed.”*

---

## Overview

**Race:** Nord (Dragonborn, heavily corrupted by Apocrypha influence)  
**Class:** Dragon Priest / Hermaeus Mora’s Former Champion / Word of Power Tyrant  

**Alignment:** Chaotic Evil  

**Affiliations:**
- Hermaeus Mora (Former Servant / Failed Champion)
- Apocrypha (Bound Exile)
- Dragon Cult of Solstheim (Ancient Leadership Legacy)
- Dragons of the Merethic Era (Domination-based allegiance, fractured)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Speaks as if reality is beneath him in hierarchy  
- Treats all other Dragonborn as “unfinished copies”  
- Displays contempt for mortal limitation and free will  
- Obsessed with ownership of power rather than mastery  

**Playstyle:**
High-tier boss archetype specializing in dragon shout domination, spell absorption, summoning, and battlefield control. Functions as a hybrid mage-dragon warrior with reality-warping Apocrypha influence.

- **Arc Name:** *The First Claim That Never Ended*
- **Status:** Deceased (Apocrypha echo remains bound to Hermaeus Mora’s realm)
- **Goal:** Escape Apocrypha’s control and reassert dominance over all Dragonborn bloodlines
- **Emotional Thread:** Pride fused with resentment—believes betrayal by Hermaeus Mora was theft of rightful divinity

- Armor fused with ancient Dragon Priest relics and unreadable tentacle-like growths

## Character Design / Theme

A man who stopped being a person and became a claim over power itself.

**Silhouette:**
- Tall, imposing, and rigidly controlled posture
- Dragon Priest armor fused with organic, reality-distorting elements

**Armor / Attire Design:**
- Ancient Dragon Priest mask inscribed with forbidden shout-languages
- Armor layered with Apocrypha-corrupted textures and shifting geometry
- Cloak or back-appendages that appear partially non-Euclidean in motion

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Deep abyss green  
- Accent: Arcane cyan-blue  
- Secondary: Burnt gold relic tones  

**Signature Details:**
- Reality distortion subtly warps around him in combat  
- Dragon runes appear and vanish across armor surfaces  
- Voice carries layered echoes of multiple overlapping identities

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Arrogant to the point of ontological superiority complex  
- Views all beings as lesser iterations of himself or failures of destiny  
- Highly intelligent but deeply corrupted by external influence  
- Treats power as entitlement rather than responsibility  

**Motivations:**
- Escape Apocrypha’s control and reclaim autonomy  
- Dominate all Dragonborn as proof of singular supremacy  
- Reassert his identity as “First” in both power and history  
- Break constraints imposed by gods, Daedra, and fate itself  

**Flaws:**
- Extreme hubris and inability to acknowledge equals  
- Overreliance on stolen power rather than earned mastery  
- Mentally fragmented by Apocrypha corruption  
- Underestimates adaptability of future Dragonborn  

**Relationships:**
- **Hermaeus Mora:** Former master; now viewed as betrayer and jailer  
- **Dragonborn (Player Character):** Final contradiction to his claimed supremacy  
- **Dragons:** Tools of domination, not allies or equals  
- **Miraak Cultists:** Extensions of influence rather than individuals  
- **Virel / Elias (conceptual resonance):** Would interpret them as “fractured power expressions” unworthy of singular dominance

## Backstory: *“The First Dragonborn Who Refused to Be Second to Anything”*

Miraak was not chosen.

He took.

In an age where dragons ruled through fear and the Thu’um defined existence itself, Miraak discovered something more dangerous than power: the ability to claim it without permission. As the first Dragonborn, he did not inherit a legacy—he defined what inheritance meant.

But power attracts attention.

Hermaeus Mora did not destroy him.

He contained him.

Bound within Apocrypha, Miraak spent ages between reality and knowledge, consuming fragments of forbidden truths and sharpening his hatred into structure. What began as imprisonment became philosophy: if he could not escape, he would return stronger than the world that betrayed him.

Every Dragonborn that followed became, to him, evidence of dilution.

Every hero became proof of regression.

And every attempt to stop him became confirmation that he was still important enough to fear.

He is not merely a villain of history.

He is the idea that power should never be shared—only taken.
