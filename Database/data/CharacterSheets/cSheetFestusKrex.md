## Background

Festus Krex spent much of his early life within Imperial academic circles, where magical study was treated as a structured discipline tied to state utility rather than personal exploration. He showed early aptitude in alchemy and conjuration, but his interests quickly diverged from sanctioned applications. While most Imperial mages focused on controlled spellcasting or battlefield utility, Festus became increasingly interested in the underlying mechanics of magical causality—specifically how substances, enchantments, and summoned entities could be combined to produce predictable lethal outcomes.

His academic career did not progress in a conventional sense. Although he demonstrated exceptional technical understanding, his research methods frequently crossed ethical and procedural boundaries. This led to increasing friction with institutional oversight, particularly as his experiments moved further into prohibited areas such as toxin refinement, necromantic adjacency studies, and uncontrolled conjuration testing.

Rather than fully expelling him, Imperial institutions distanced themselves from his work, effectively isolating him within correspondence-level research channels where his findings could be observed without granting him direct authority or access to regulated facilities. This period reinforced a pattern of intellectual independence and institutional mistrust, shaping his belief that meaningful magical advancement occurs outside bureaucratic constraint.

Festus eventually severed formal ties with Imperial academia and entered the darker edges of Tamriel’s magical underworld, where experimentation was not restricted by law or ethical committee. His transition into the Dark Brotherhood was not ideological. He did not join out of loyalty to Sithis or belief in assassination doctrine. Instead, he recognized the organization as a stable and continuous supply of real-world applications for his research.

Within the Brotherhood, Festus established himself as a specialist in indirect lethality. His work focused on refining methods of assassination that reduced risk to the assassin while increasing certainty of outcome. This included poison delivery systems, environmental manipulation, and the integration of conjured entities into assassination protocols.

Festus does not view death as a moral event. To him, it is a measurable result produced by interacting systems. This perspective places him at odds with more ideologically driven members of the Brotherhood, but his utility ensures continued tolerance. Over time, he became less of a traditional assassin and more of a logistical architect of elimination methods.

In practice, Festus operates as both researcher and implementer. He tests theoretical constructs through contracted killings, refines outcomes based on observed failure conditions, and adjusts his methodologies accordingly. His loyalty lies not with individuals, but with the continuity of experimentation itself.

Within the broader structure of the Dark Brotherhood, Festus Krex represents controlled unpredictability: a mind capable of innovation in lethal systems, bound only loosely by organizational necessity and sustained primarily by the availability of new variables to study.
