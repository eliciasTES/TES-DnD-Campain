# Cicero — Character Sheet

## Overview

**Race:** Imperial  
**Class:** Assassin / Jester Courier / Keeper of the Night Mother  
**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral (leaning Chaotic Evil in execution patterns)  

**Affiliations:**
- Dark Brotherhood (Keeper of the Night Mother)
- Night Mother’s Sarcophagus (Primary Custodian)
- Black Sacrament Tradition (Fanatical Interpreter)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Speaks in erratic, shifting tones ranging from playful to threatening without transition  
- Treats contracts and murder as performance rather than procedure  
- Displays extreme emotional volatility tied to perceived disrespect of the Night Mother  
- Laughs or jokes in situations others interpret as life-threatening  

**Playstyle:**
High-mobility stealth assassin archetype specializing in unpredictability, psychological disruption, and close-range elimination. Functions as a chaos-driven operative whose effectiveness comes from instability rather than discipline.

---

## Appearance

- Thin, pale Imperial with exaggerated facial expressions and restless movement patterns  
- Green jester outfit reinforced with hidden assassin tools and light armor padding  
- Painted or stylized facial markings that emphasize expressions during speech  
- Constantly animated posture, rarely still even when idle  
- Presence feels unpredictable, as if attention cannot settle on him for long  

---

## Character Design / Theme

An assassin who replaced discipline with performance, and structure with ritualized madness.

**Silhouette:**
- Lean, flexible frame optimized for erratic motion and sudden direction changes  
- Movement pattern is irregular rather than controlled  

**Armor / Attire Design:**
- Traditional jester clothing modified with concealed weapon systems  
- Lightweight leather reinforcement under fabric layers  
- Hidden blades integrated into sleeves, boots, and glove seams  
- Bells and ornamentation used both as misdirection and psychological pressure  

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Dark green  
- Accent: Bone white  
- Secondary: Faded gold and worn leather tones  

**Signature Details:**
- Weapons often concealed within theatrical props or costume elements  
- Constant low-level motion even during conversation  
- Visual presentation is intentionally distracting to observers  

---

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Highly unstable emotional expression  
- Deeply devoted to the Night Mother in a personal, distorted interpretation  
- Alternates rapidly between friendliness, fear, humor, and aggression  
- Interprets assassination as ritual performance rather than contract work  

**Motivations:**
- Preserve the sanctity and authority of the Night Mother  
- Ensure the Dark Brotherhood continues to receive contracts  
- Maintain personal role as Keeper within the Brotherhood hierarchy  
- Validate his interpretation of “the Family” as sacred and absolute  

**Flaws:**
- Severe emotional instability and unpredictable behavior patterns  
- Difficulty distinguishing social interaction from ritual performance  
- Prone to violent escalation when feeling dismissed or ignored  
- Lack of operational discipline compared to other Brotherhood members

## Background

Cicero began his life within the Dark Brotherhood already shaped by instability and isolation. From an early age, he was drawn to the Night Mother not as a symbolic figure within the Brotherhood’s doctrine, but as a literal presence that demanded attention, obedience, and care. This fixation became the defining structure of his identity long before he assumed the role of Keeper.

Before becoming Keeper of the Night Mother, Cicero served as a courier for the Black Sacrament network, transporting contracts and messages between scattered Brotherhood sanctuaries. In this role, he was exposed to the fragmented and declining state of the organization, witnessing repeated betrayals, dissolutions, and internal conflicts. These experiences reinforced his belief that the Brotherhood’s survival depended less on leadership or structure and more on unwavering devotion to the Night Mother herself.

When the position of Keeper became vacant, Cicero accepted the role with absolute conviction. He did not see it as a promotion or responsibility, but as a sacred continuation of his life’s purpose. The Night Mother was not, in his view, a relic or ceremonial corpse, but the living center of the Brotherhood’s identity. This belief set him apart even from other deeply religious members, as his devotion bordered on total psychological identification with her will.

Cicero’s time as Keeper coincided with a period of internal fracture within the Dark Brotherhood. As Astrid’s leadership attempted to reshape the organization into a more pragmatic and independent structure, Cicero became increasingly isolated. He viewed these reforms not as adaptation, but as heresy against the Night Mother’s authority. This ideological divide gradually escalated into paranoia, reinforcing his belief that the Brotherhood had lost its way and required correction.

Over time, Cicero’s behavior became more erratic, shaped by prolonged isolation, grief over perceived decline, and unwavering fixation on preserving the Night Mother’s sanctity. He interpreted any deviation from traditional rites as existential threat to the Brotherhood’s existence. This perspective placed him in direct conflict with other members, who viewed him as unstable, unpredictable, and dangerous to organizational cohesion.

Despite this, Cicero remains one of the most dedicated individuals within the Dark Brotherhood. His loyalty is not directed toward leadership, contracts, or even the organization itself, but toward what he believes the Brotherhood is meant to be. In his mind, he is not a servant of chaos, but the last true guardian of its original purpose.
