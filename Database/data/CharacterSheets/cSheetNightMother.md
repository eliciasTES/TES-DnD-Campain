# The Night Mother — Character Sheet

## Overview

**Race:** Undead (Preserved Human Remains / Unknown Spiritual Entity)  
**Class:** Daedric Proxy / Assassin Covenant Anchor / Death-Doctrine Vessel  

**Alignment:** Neutral Evil (metaphysical influence; non-corporeal intent expression)  

**Affiliations:**
- Dark Brotherhood (Central Religious Figure)
- Sithis (Metaphysical Patron Force)
- Black Sacrament Network (Invocation Anchor)
- Void of Silence (Conceptual Domain of Deathly Will)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Does not speak directly except through the Listener  
- Presence is perceived rather than observed  
- Commands are interpreted rather than clearly stated  
- Exists as both ritual object and spiritual authority simultaneously  

**Playstyle:**
Non-combat metaphysical entity archetype functioning as a quest-giver, directive force, and organizational anchor. Influences assassins indirectly through interpretation, prophecy-like requests, and ritual communication.

- Preserved, mummified female form housed in a sealed coffin  
- Appearance is static and unchanging regardless of time passage  
- Surrounded by ritual bindings, cloth wrappings, and ceremonial preservation elements  
- Often depicted as both physical remains and abstract presence simultaneously  
- Presence feels heavy, silent, and spatially dominant despite lack of motion

## Background

The Night Mother’s origins are not recorded in any reliable historical account, and what remains is a mixture of myth, fragmented testimony, and ritualized belief maintained by the Dark Brotherhood itself. What is known is that she was once a living woman who became central to the formation of Sithis-worshiping assassination covenants, eventually transforming into the symbolic and spiritual core of the Dark Brotherhood.

After her death, her body did not return to the world in the conventional sense of decay or memory. Instead, it was preserved through unknown means and placed within a sealed coffin, where it became the focal point of a new system of communication between Sithis and mortal assassins. Over time, this system stabilized into what is now known as the Black Sacrament tradition, where the Night Mother serves as the intermediary between contract and execution.

Her physical remains were entrusted to the earliest iterations of the Dark Brotherhood, establishing a structure in which assassins no longer acted independently under loose interpretation of Sithis, but instead received directed contracts through a singular conduit. This shifted the Brotherhood from scattered cult-like cells into a more unified, ritual-bound organization.

The identity of the Night Mother as an individual has been gradually replaced by her function within the Brotherhood. Few within the organization treat her as a historical person with a lived past; instead, she is understood primarily as an instrument of will and communication. Even her original life is largely irrelevant to most members, except as a point of curiosity or theological speculation.

Within the Dark Brotherhood, her presence has remained constant across generations of assassins, sanctuaries, and leadership structures. Whether through divine influence, metaphysical persistence, or ritual reinforcement, she continues to serve as the anchor of the Brotherhood’s contract system.

Despite her lack of direct interaction with the world, the Night Mother remains the defining authority of the Dark Brotherhood. Every contract, every Listener, and every interpretation of Sithis ultimately traces back to her presence, whether understood literally or symbolically.
