# Aela the Huntress — Character Sheet

## Overview
**Race:** Nord  
**Class:** Werewolf Hunter / Circle Warrior / Beast-Blood Skirmisher  

**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral (leaning Neutral Good in loyalty-driven contexts)  

**Affiliations:**
- The Companions (Circle Member)
- Harbinger Kodlak Whitemane (Loyal to leadership, not ideology)
- Werewolf Bloodline (Voluntary Beast-Blood Bearer)
- Whiterun Hunting Traditions (Informal cultural influence)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Treats conversation as secondary to action and observation  
- Frequently evaluates people as if assessing prey movement patterns  
- Shows little interest in abstract moral debate  
- Displays calm intensity even in emotionally charged situations  

**Playstyle:**
High-mobility melee skirmisher specializing in stealth hunting, beast form combat, tracking, and rapid engagement. Excels in pursuit, ambush, and sustained combat in wild terrain.

---

## Appearance
- Athletic Nord build optimized for endurance and speed  
- Long red hair typically kept practical for combat and tracking  
- Sharp, alert eyes that constantly scan for movement and threat signals  
- Light Companions armor adapted for mobility and wilderness traversal  
- Presence feels predatory even in stillness  

---

## Character Design / Theme

A hunter who does not distinguish between survival and purpose—only between what moves and what stops moving.

**Silhouette:**
- Lean, agile frame designed for rapid directional shifts  
- Relaxed stance that can transition instantly into pursuit or attack  

**Armor / Attire Design:**
- Light-to-medium Companions armor optimized for mobility  
- Wolf iconography subtly present but not ceremonial  
- Fur and leather elements suited for wilderness tracking  
- Minimal ornamental design to avoid encumbering movement  

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Forest brown  
- Accent: Companions steel gray  
- Secondary: Nordic red tones  

**Signature Details:**
- Movement always deliberate, even when appearing casual  
- Subtle signs of beast transformation resilience in posture and stance  
- Carries hunting tools as extensions of instinct rather than equipment  

---

## Personality

**Traits:**
- Highly observant and instinct-driven  
- Direct, blunt, and minimally expressive in speech  
- Comfortable with violence as a natural system of the world  
- Loyal to individuals she respects rather than institutions  

**Motivations:**
- Perfect the balance between human discipline and beast instinct  
- Prove that strength is clarity, not morality  
- Follow the hunt wherever it leads, without hesitation  
- Maintain loyalty to the Circle through shared experience, not ideology  

**Flaws:**
- Emotional detachment from non-essential relationships  
- Difficulty engaging with abstract moral or philosophical concerns  
- Over-identification with instinctual decision-making  
- Limited long-term strategic thinking outside immediate objectives

## Backstory: “The Hunt Is the Only Truth That Doesn’t Lie”

Aela the Huntress was born into the wilds of Skyrim and shaped long before she ever entered Jorrvaskr. Her earliest life was defined by survival rather than structure, learning to track, hunt, and endure in environments where hesitation meant failure. Unlike many who join the Companions through formal invitation or lineage, Aela’s place within the Circle came from capability proven in the field rather than reputation within a city.

She was raised among hunters of the Reach and later drawn into the Companions, where her skills were immediately recognized. Even within Jorrvaskr, she never fully adopted its sense of domestic stability or tradition. Instead, she remained oriented toward the wild world outside its walls, treating civilization as temporary shelter rather than a defining identity.

Aela is one of the Circle and fully embraces the beast blood of lycanthropy. Unlike others who struggle with control or conflict, she views the transformation as a return to a more honest state of being. For her, the divide between man and beast is not a moral or philosophical issue, but a natural extension of existence. This belief shapes much of how she approaches both combat and identity.

Within the Companions, Aela functions as both hunter and guide. She is often the one sent to track targets, assess terrain, and operate in environments where precision and patience are required. Her understanding of Skyrim’s wilderness is not theoretical but lived, built from constant movement through forests, tundra, and predator territory. She trusts instinct over planning, especially when the situation is fluid or unpredictable.

Aela does not place strong importance on formal leadership or political structure. She respects strength, independence, and the ability to act without hesitation. Because of this, she is often skeptical of hesitation or over-analysis in others, viewing it as a liability in situations where survival depends on immediate response.

Her loyalty to the Companions is rooted in shared experience rather than ideology. She does not view them as an institution to be preserved, but as a pack—something defined by those within it rather than rules imposed from above. This perspective shapes her relationships with other members of Jorrvaskr, particularly those who rely more heavily on structure or tradition.

In the broader world, Aela does not concern herself with political factions or metaphysical systems unless they directly affect her hunt or her pack. She is aware of figures and conflicts that move beyond Skyrim’s wilderness, but she filters them through practical relevance rather than abstract significance.

Aela does not seek to explain the world or define her place within it. She moves through it as a hunter, responding to what is present, adapting to what changes, and surviving alongside those she considers part of her pack.
