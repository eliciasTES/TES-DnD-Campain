## Overview Babette

**Race:** Breton (Vampire)  
**Class:** Alchemical Assassin / Eternal Child Archetype / Poison Specialist  

**Alignment:** Neutral Evil (leaning Chaotic Neutral in behavior)  

**Affiliations:**
- Dark Brotherhood (Field Assassin / Senior Operative)
- The Black Sacrament Network (Indirect Contact Through Contracts)
- Cyrodiilic Vampire Lineage (Undisclosed Origin)
- Independent Alchemical Research Circles (Self-directed experimentation)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Maintains a childlike demeanor that contrasts sharply with her profession  
- Treats assassination work with casual curiosity rather than emotional weight  
- Shows fascination with fear responses in targets and observers  
- Often behaves as though violence is part of play rather than labor  

**Playstyle:**
High-mobility stealth assassin specializing in poison application, distraction tactics, and rapid elimination. Excels in chaotic environments where unpredictability can be leveraged to avoid detection and maximize lethality.

## Background


Babette was born in Cyrodiil several centuries ago during a period of political instability and frequent conflict between noble houses. Her early life is no longer fully distinct in her own memory, not due to trauma in the conventional sense, but because of the length of time she has existed since then. What remains consistent in her recollection is that she was not always what she is now, and that her transformation into a vampire occurred at an age when she was still physically a child.

The circumstances of her vampirism are tied to survival rather than choice. She was turned during a period where vampiric activity in Cyrodiil operated through small, hidden covens that rarely left records or witnesses. Unlike many who are turned later in life, Babette’s transformation froze her physical development while allowing her mind and intellect to continue progressing over centuries. This created a permanent disconnect between appearance and lived experience.

Over time, Babette learned to use her form as an advantage rather than a limitation. Her appearance allowed her to move through environments without being perceived as a threat, while her vampiric condition provided the longevity and resilience to refine her skills across generations. She developed an interest in alchemy and poisons early, initially as a means of survival, but later as a deliberate craft.

Her entry into the Dark Brotherhood came through recognition of utility rather than recruitment in the traditional sense. The Brotherhood provided structure, resources, and targets that allowed her to refine her methods in a controlled environment. Unlike many members, she did not join for ideology or necessity alone. She joined because it allowed her to continue her work with consistent access to real-world applications.

Within the Brotherhood, Babette became a specialist in poisoning, subtle elimination, and psychological misdirection. Her methods rely on being underestimated, allowing her to approach targets without triggering immediate defensive responses. She uses her appearance strategically, understanding how perception shapes vulnerability.

Babette does not define herself by her vampirism in emotional terms. It is a condition she has adapted to over time, shaping her approach to mortality and human behavior. She maintains a detached curiosity toward mortal life, not from cruelty alone, but from long-term observation of patterns that repeat across generations.

In the broader structure of the Dark Brotherhood, Babette functions as both an asset and an anomaly. She bridges the gap between innocence and lethality, using her form as a tool of misdirection while maintaining complete competence in assassination craft. Her continued existence within the Brotherhood is less a matter of acceptance and more a recognition that she has become one of its most efficient and unconventional operatives.
