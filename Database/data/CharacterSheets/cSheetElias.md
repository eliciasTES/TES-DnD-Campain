🔥 Elias Venym – The Silver Thorn

> *“Light does not absolve. It only reveals what still festers beneath.”*

---

## Overview

**Race:** Imperial (dark-skinned)  
**Class:** Spellsword / Inquisitorial Hybrid  
**Alignment:** Lawful Neutral leaning Chaotic Good  

**Affiliations:**
- Former Namiran Cultist
- Independent Daedra Hunter
- Meridia’s Chosen (Anointed Champion)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Speaks softly before executions, as if praying or apologizing  
- Never fully sheathes his blade—keeps it “half-ready”  
- Avoids eating in the presence of others  

**Playstyle:**
Balanced executioner—blends radiant anti-undead magic with stealth assassination and brutal close-quarters dueling. Excels at adaptive combat, moral ambiguity, and target elimination in layered environments.

**Color Palette:**
- Primary: Deep obsidian-gray

## Signature Abilities

**Consecrated Break**  
→ A focused combat technique where a Vigilant channels Restoration energy directly through their weapon at the moment of impact. The strike briefly disrupts necrotic energy, weakens Daedric bindings, and interrupts active enchantments or minor spellcasting.

It is not a destructive blast—it is a correction. The ability is taught to ensure Vigilants can reliably stop undead regeneration, dispel weak curses, and prevent ritual momentum from stabilizing in combat.

However, it requires precise timing and physical commitment. Miss the moment of impact, and the effect fails entirely. Used properly, it turns a single clean strike into a decisive interruption of unnatural forces. Used poorly, it is just a heavier swing.

**Sanctum Pulse**  
→ A short-range outward burst of consecrated energy emitted through prayer-anchored focus. The pulse does not deal significant damage but temporarily disrupts hostile magical influence within a small radius.

Its primary functions are:

- breaking concentration of hostile spellcasters
- weakening summoned or bound entities
- revealing invisible or illusion-disguised targets
- briefly suppressing necromantic control over nearby undead

The effect is intentionally limited. Vigilants are taught that overextending divine force risks backlash and spiritual exhaustion.

Sanctum Pulse is therefore used as a control tool, not a finishing move—meant to create breathing room in overwhelming encounters or to reset a corrupted battlefield.

## Ultimate Ability

### Hollow Feast

→ Elias invokes a forbidden ritual gleaned from years of studying Namiran cults, briefly awakening the unseen burden of suffering that clings to every living soul. An oppressive veil settles over the battlefield as exhaustion, grief, fear, lingering wounds, and self-doubt rise to the surface of those caught within its reach.

The ritual inflicts little direct harm, but it drastically weakens an enemy's ability to fight. Movements become sluggish, muscles feel impossibly heavy, and even seasoned warriors struggle to maintain their footing. Spellcasters find their concentration constantly interrupted as intrusive whispers and painful memories fracture their focus, causing complex rituals to falter and powerful spells to collapse before completion.

To Elias, however, the battlefield becomes calm. As enemies slow beneath the weight of their own suffering, he gains precious moments to accomplish objectives that brute force could never achieve. He can slip past hostile lines, recover a relic before a ritual is completed, rescue civilians trapped behind enemy forces, dismantle Daedric altars, or simply withdraw from an otherwise hopeless battle.

The technique excels against large groups and magically coordinated enemies, transforming overwhelming encounters into manageable ones without requiring Elias to defeat every opponent. Against a single powerful foe, however, its effects are far less pronounced, as exceptional strength of will or divine power can partially resist the ritual's influence.

Though Elias never embraces Namira's teachings, invoking Hollow Feast requires him to briefly immerse himself in the collective misery surrounding him. When the ritual ends, fragments of that pain linger within his own mind, leaving him emotionally drained and haunted by memories and emotions that are not his own. The more often he relies upon the technique, the greater the risk that another's suffering begins to feel indistinguishable from his own.

VERSION 1 - Namrian Devotee to Exlied Traitor

## Backstory: *“Between Light and Bone”*

Elias never knew an ordinary childhood.

He was raised within a secluded cult devoted to Namira, where decay was revered as truth and suffering was celebrated as enlightenment. From the moment he could walk, he was taught that beauty was a lie, compassion was weakness, and that every living thing would eventually return to rot. Rituals, sacrifice, and isolation were simply everyday life. Elias accepted these teachings because he knew nothing else.

Yet something never fit.

Unlike the others, Elias questioned why suffering had to be created rather than endured. He obeyed the cult's commands, but he found himself secretly sparing animals meant for sacrifice, helping prisoners survive longer than intended, and wondering why the cult spoke so confidently about truths they seemed unwilling to question themselves.

During one pilgrimage, Elias encountered the ruins of an ancient shrine dedicated to Meridia. While the other cultists felt only revulsion, Elias experienced something entirely different. The light emanating from the forgotten temple did not burn him. Instead, it exposed every lie he had accepted as truth. For the first time in his life, he understood that he had been raised inside a prison disguised as purpose.

Leaving the cult meant abandoning the only family he had ever known.

The cult branded him a traitor and hunted him relentlessly across Skyrim. Alone and possessing little more than the survival skills his former masters had taught him, Elias survived as a wandering hunter. Meridia's influence slowly grew stronger, not because she erased his past, but because she demanded he confront it.

Ironically, everything the cult taught him became useful against them. Their rituals, hiding places, methods of infiltration, and understanding of Daedric corruption became the very weapons he used to dismantle cults across Tamriel.

Meridia eventually chose Elias as one of her champions—not because he was pure, but because he willingly carried the burden of confronting darkness without pretending he stood above it.

By the time he meets Virel, Elias has become a wandering hunter of cults, necromancers, vampires, and Daedra. He knows better than anyone how corruption begins because he once called it home.




VERSION 2 - Vigliant to Cult Infiltrator

# Backstory: *"The Thorn Between Dawn and Dusk"*

Elias was born in the heart of the Empire to an ordinary family that valued honest work, discipline, and service. Though his childhood was unremarkable, he grew up hearing stories of Daedric cults, necromancers, vampires, and the countless horrors that preyed upon the innocent beyond the safety of city walls. To him, the world seemed simple. There were good people and evil people. Those who served the Divines protected life, while those who served the Daedra sought only to corrupt and destroy it.

Driven by that conviction, Elias joined the Vigilants of Stendarr while still a young man. Their purpose resonated deeply with him: protect the innocent, destroy the unnatural, and stand unwavering against the influence of Daedra. He embraced their discipline with enthusiasm, believing righteousness required certainty. Every mission reinforced the lessons he had been taught. Necromancers defiled the dead. Vampires preyed upon the living. Daedric cults spread suffering wherever they took root. In those early years, Elias never questioned the righteousness of his order.

As his skill and judgment grew, the Vigilants entrusted him with more dangerous assignments. One such mission would change the course of his life.

Rumors had surfaced of a hidden cult devoted to Namira operating in the hills outside a small settlement. Rather than launching an immediate assault, Elias was ordered to infiltrate the cult, identify its leaders, and gather enough information to destroy the organization from within.

He entered expecting monsters.

Instead, he found people.

The gathering consisted of widows abandoned by their families, miners crippled by disease, veterans scarred by forgotten wars, beggars shunned by society, and laborers whose suffering had driven them to seek comfort wherever they could find it. Many spoke of Namira not as a goddess of cruelty, but as the only divine power that accepted those the rest of Tamriel had discarded. They were not plotting conquest or murder. They simply wanted to belong somewhere.

For the first time, Elias found himself questioning everything he believed.

As weeks turned into months, he carefully distinguished between the ordinary followers and the cult's inner circle. Hidden beneath the quiet fellowship were priests who manipulated despair for their own gain. They encouraged cannibalism, ritual sacrifice, and acts of degradation that twisted vulnerable people into willing instruments of Namira's darker desires. Elias came to understand that the cult's greatest danger was not its desperate followers, but the leaders who preyed upon that desperation.

When the time came to act, Elias secretly guided many innocent followers to safety before exposing the cult's true leadership. Together with a small contingent of Vigilants, he struck down the priests responsible for the cult's atrocities and dismantled their operations.

But his mercy did not go unnoticed.

When his superiors learned that he had deliberately spared members of the cult, they accused him of compromising his mission. To them, anyone who willingly knelt before a Daedric Prince was beyond redemption. Elias argued that justice required discernment—that punishing the guilty and slaughtering the desperate were not the same thing—but his words fell on deaf ears.

Whether he formally resigned or was quietly cast out hardly mattered. Elias left the Vigilants knowing he could no longer serve an order that judged symbols more readily than people.

Without the structure that had defined his life, Elias began wandering Tamriel alone. Rather than simply hunting Daedric cults, he sought to understand them. He studied forgotten shrines, translated ancient texts, questioned priests, spoke with pilgrims, and observed cults from within before deciding whether they posed a genuine threat. He came to believe that every Daedric Prince embodied a fundamental truth about existence, but that those truths became dangerous when elevated above compassion and reason.

Among them all, Namira fascinated him most.

She represented decay, suffering, rejection, and the uncomfortable realities that every society preferred to hide. Elias accepted that suffering was an unavoidable part of mortal life, but rejected the belief that suffering should be celebrated or deliberately inflicted. To understand darkness, he concluded, was not the same as serving it.

It was during these years that Meridia's attention turned toward him.

Unlike many of her followers, Elias did not fight darkness because he feared it or because he believed himself untouched by it. He fought it because he understood its appeal. He had seen how loneliness, despair, and rejection could lead ordinary people into the arms of terrible powers. He knew evil rarely announced itself openly; it often arrived wearing the face of comfort, belonging, or necessity.

Meridia saw in Elias a rare kind of champion—one who would neither underestimate darkness nor be consumed by it. She offered him no promises of purity or absolution. Instead, she entrusted him with a purpose: to hunt those who twisted life, death, and suffering into instruments of corruption, while never forgetting the difference between those who chose evil and those who had simply lost their way.

Elias accepted, but not as a servant bound by unquestioning devotion.

He would follow no order, temple, or prince blindly ever again.

He became a wandering spellsword, investigator, and hunter of cults, answering only to his own conscience. Though Meridia's light guides his hand against undead, Daedra, and those who prey upon the helpless, he carries with him the lessons learned in Namira's shadows. Every battle reminds him that corruption is rarely born from monsters alone. More often, it begins with ordinary people searching for hope in the wrong places.

When he eventually meets Virel, Elias recognizes a familiar struggle. She, too, has been judged by powers larger than herself and forced into exile by circumstances few could understand. Rather than seeing another soul marked by darkness, he sees someone searching for the same truth that has guided his own journey: that neither light nor shadow defines a person—only the choices they make when confronted by both.
