## Overview

**Race:** Nord (Werewolf)  
**Class:** Pack Enforcer / Stealth Assassin / Physical Disruption Specialist  

**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral (leaning Lawful Neutral in loyalty)  

**Affiliations:**
- Dark Brotherhood (Senior Assassin / Astrid’s Enforcer)
- Falkreath Werewolf Bloodline (Afflicted, Controlled Transformation)
- Brotherhood Internal Security (Unofficial Role)

**Notable Quirks:**
- Speaks rarely, preferring action over explanation  
- Treats threats as problems to be removed, not debated  
- Displays unusual patience until violence becomes necessary  
- Shows strong behavioral shift between calm and combat states  

**Playstyle:**
High-impact melee assassin specializing in stealth infiltration followed by overwhelming close-range disruption. Excels in eliminating priority targets quickly and escaping unstable situations through brute force transformation.

## Background

Arnbjorn grew up in Falkreath Hold, in a region where dense forest, scattered settlements, and frequent conflict between bandit groups made survival dependent on physical strength and awareness rather than law or structure. From an early age, he learned to rely on instinct and endurance, developing a preference for direct solutions over negotiation or hesitation.

He did not come from a formal military background, but his natural strength and temperament placed him in roles that required physical enforcement. Before joining the Dark Brotherhood, he lived as a solitary mercenary and hunter, taking work that involved tracking dangerous targets in wilderness terrain. This period of his life reinforced a pattern of behavior centered on patience, observation, and sudden violence when conditions aligned.

Arnbjorn’s connection to lycanthropy was not sought out deliberately. It emerged as part of the broader presence of werewolf influence in Skyrim’s wild regions, where infection is often the result of proximity rather than intention. The transformation altered his relationship with violence, but did not fundamentally change his behavioral structure. Instead, it amplified what was already present: a controlled, restrained individual capable of extreme force when necessary.

His entry into the Dark Brotherhood came through Astrid, who recognized his reliability in situations requiring physical enforcement and operational security. Unlike many assassins who rely on subtlety or manipulation, Arnbjorn provided a stabilizing counterbalance: he could be deployed in situations where discretion was no longer required and failure meant escalation.

Within the Brotherhood, Arnbjorn did not pursue rank or influence. He developed a consistent role as Astrid’s enforcer, trusted to handle threats that could not be managed through contracts alone. His loyalty to her became the defining structure of his place within the organization, shaping both his decisions and his identity within the Brotherhood’s internal hierarchy.

Arnbjorn’s relationship with his lycanthropy is functional rather than philosophical. He does not view it as a curse or gift in ideological terms. Instead, he treats it as a condition that must be controlled and directed. This pragmatic acceptance aligns closely with his overall approach to life: problems are not interpreted, they are managed.

In the broader context of the Dark Brotherhood, Arnbjorn represents stability through force. He does not shape policy or doctrine, but he ensures that when structure fails or is threatened, there is always someone capable of responding without hesitation.
