## Backstory: *“She Who Answered Before She Asked”*

The Caller is a Breton mage whose pursuit of forbidden arcane systems led her into partial resonance with Apocrypha-linked phenomena and unstable summoning methodologies. Once affiliated with the College of Winterhold, she was expelled after repeated incidents involving unauthorized experimentation with memory-binding magic, recursive summoning frameworks, and cognitively invasive spell structures.

Her early career began in conventional arcane study, where she demonstrated exceptional aptitude for theoretical alteration magic and illusion-based cognition manipulation. Unlike most mages, she showed an unusual interest in the structure of knowledge itself—not just how spells worked, but how ideas persisted, mutated, and resisted forgetting.

This curiosity led her toward experimental research into memory-binding enchantments and communicative summoning techniques. Her hypothesis was simple but dangerous: that magic is not only cast, but responded to—that certain systems of arcane practice function less like commands and more like queries answered by something beyond the caster’s direct control.

At first, these experiments produced subtle anomalies: delayed spell echoes, inconsistent memory recall, and localized distortions in written records. Over time, however, the effects escalated. Her spellwork began exhibiting signs of external influence—responses that did not originate from her own intent, but from something interacting with her arcane output.

Rather than interpreting this as failure, she treated it as confirmation.

Her work eventually intersected with Apocrypha-adjacent residual phenomena, whether through deliberate summoning experimentation or unintended resonance is unclear even within surviving records. What is consistent is that her magic began to exhibit “answer structures”—spell outcomes that behaved as if they were partially authored by an external intelligence rather than fully generated by her will.

This shift fundamentally altered her perception of identity. She began recording conversations that no one else remembered, writing in languages that altered when observed, and documenting “interruptions” in thought that she attributed to external cognitive interaction. Whether these are actual communications or interpretations of arcane instability remains unresolved.

Her expulsion from the College of Winterhold was framed as a disciplinary action regarding unsafe magical research. In practice, it was a containment decision—an attempt to prevent further destabilization of institutional memory records and arcane safety protocols.

Following her expulsion, The Caller continued her work independently, assembling fragmented circles of similarly displaced or curious scholars. These groups function less as formal organizations and more as distributed research nodes focused on understanding unstable knowledge systems, particularly those tied to Apocrypha resonance and memory corruption phenomena.

Over time, her relationship with magic ceased to resemble traditional spellcasting. Instead, she began describing her practice as “calling,” a method of initiating structured queries into unknown arcane systems and interpreting their responses. This methodology prioritizes outcomes over authorship, treating magical effects as negotiated results rather than direct expressions of will.

Her personality reflects this shift. She no longer distinguishes cleanly between internal thought and external response, instead treating cognition as a shared or permeable system. This makes her exceptionally adaptive in arcane environments but increasingly detached from conventional notions of selfhood.

When the campaign begins, The Caller operates at the margins of established magical institutions, pursuing unstable knowledge systems that most organizations refuse to acknowledge. Her goal is not power in the traditional sense, but comprehension of whether magic is truly an extension of the self—or something that has been answering all along.
